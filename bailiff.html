Notice of Enforcement

You did NOT get a NOTICE OF ENFORCEMENT before bailiffs attended.

The law says bailiffs must give debtors a Notice of Enforcement,[1] at least seven clear days before taking control of goods, or take a money or taking control of goods.[2]

If you did not get a notice of enforcement, it is probably because you recently moved, your vehicle is registered to a different address or, the bailiff company did not give you one.

If the bailiff cannot show compelling evidence he gave you a Notice of Enforcement, then the enforcement is not compliant, and the bailiff may not recover fees and charges.[3]

You may recover any fees and charges taken, and claim damages,[4] together with your costs.[5]
